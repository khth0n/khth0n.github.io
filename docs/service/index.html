Past Service Projects

Holiday and City Event Volunteering

During high school, I volunteered for the City of Leander's Trunk-or-Treat and Round Rock's Christmas Dreams & Snowy Things events regularly. I would also attend STEM nights held at various middle schools within or near the City of Leander to encourage middle school students to get interested in the STEM programs that would be available to them to take in high school. I particularly liked to represent my school as a STEM student because I felt I helped to alleviate and disprove many of the stereotypes and fears young middle school students had regarding their educational abilities.

Volunteering for School-related Functions

Also during high school, I volunteered for a number of events as a representative of my school. I often would act as a greeter and cleaner for my high school's theatre performances, help organize and contribute to the annual club parades, and assist the administration as an office aide during free periods and acting as a member of the school's technical support system for fixing student laptops. As a member of the National Honor Society, I also participated in most of the service projects my chapter participated in.

Future Service Projects

Fundraising, Mentoring, or Volunteering for Local Robotics Programs

Having experienced what it was like to participate in a robotics program with little funding, I know how discouraging it can be to even the most passionate communities. For this reason, I would like to volunteer to teach participants about developing software for robots in a beginner-friendly and intuitive fashion. Additionally, I believe providing my perspective as someone who learned how to program through trial-and-error initially might also allow me to relate to many participants. Whether it be speaking about various issues I recall having, helping students with difficult debugging, advertising to promote and fundraise for the program, or simply refereeing matches, I would like the opportunity to make myself an accessible resource for the future software developers to learn from.
